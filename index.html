Biography

I am a Post Doctoral Research Associate in the Department of Electrical Engineering, University of Southern California under the advisement of Dr Viktor Prasanna. I work on a diverse set of research areas which include developing deterministic and stochastic optimization algorithms and their approximations for net load balancing in smart grids, accelerating graph anlaytics on cloud platforms with heterogeneous nodes and accelerating AI algorithms on heterogeneous platforms. I received my PhD in Computer Engineering from the University of Southern California in August 2018.

Research Interests

Approximation Algorithms, Convex and Combinatorial Optimization, Reinforcement Learning, Graph Analytics Acceleration, Accelerating AI, Parallel Algorithms

Dissertation

The focus of my PhD dissertation was to develop polynomial runtime complexity approximation algorithms to perform cost optimal supply demand matching in smart grids targeting various objective functions and constraints such as cost minimization, fairness, network constraints etc. Using both theoretical analysis and practical evaluations, we showed that our supply demand matching algorithms provide solutions which are close to optimal in a small amount of time.
